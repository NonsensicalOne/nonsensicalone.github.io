We live in a cruel world where technology is exponentially rising. New technologies to use, new trends to follow, new videos to watch, new political mess being created by politicians, and our time being more important than ever. We waste more of our time on stuff that will give us nothing. But we have to ask: "Why are we wasting our time on stuff that will give us nothing?"

## Why are we wasting our time on stuff that will give us nothing?
One word. **Dopamine.** Earning "something" from doing small work triggers our body to release dopamine. Which makes us want to do it even more. Which leads people to being obsessed over it. One common example for this is Duolingo. Duolingo itself can't teach a language. Yes, it can make you learn the common words that is being used in the language but it will not teach the grammar and the special rules of the language.

Now since we have found out why we are wasting our time, it's time for the solution: **Basicifying.**

## What is basicifying?
Basicifying (as the name implies) is to make stuff seem more basic. For example: Let's make an example about Genshin Impact since its community is too much obsessed on it in my opinion. If I told a Genshin Impact player to describe their game they would likely say "An anime game with cute anime girls and I love it", now if you asked me to basicify it, it would be "A 3D game where you have to pay for 3D models". Now if I were to ask you which one sounds more interesting you would likely say the 1st one. 

And it's not just games and apps, it's our real life habits as well. For example: "Buying new clothes for myself" becomes "spending my money for a piece of fabric that people will forget about in less than a month." "Following social media accounts of people I am a fan of" becomes "watching people who have nothing to do with me doing cool things while I am sitting and watching them." That is the number one rule of basicifying: "Make things sound less desiring and more boring."

## Second rule: What you should basicify?
Anything that you think is wasting your time (or anything else) and giving you nothing in return. For example: games that just give you some virtual currency, expensive merch that has no purpose other than staying on your bookshelf, going to events or activities just because they're trendy and not because you enjoy them, binge-watching TV shows or YouTube videos that don't teach you anything, and so on.

## Third rule: What NOT to basicify?
Anything that gives you something you really need in return: Job that gives you the money to live better, partner who morally and mentally supports you, family who also mentally supports you (and doesn't use you for money), games that you earn money from (and I mean streaming and making content about it, not those ones who give 1 dollar because you spent 100 hours on their game), etc.

## Conclusion
Time is getting more important and you should take care of it. I don't have to tell you about it and you don't need to read a blog post to learn your time is very important. Cut off things that just ruin your life. If you can't cut it, try to reduce its effects. If you can't reduce its effects, try to ask for help from others who have experienced the situation you were in. If you can't do that too, don't lose your hope. Everything will be good.
